# Definition

Surfactants = **Surface Active Agents**
“Surface active” = surfactant molecules adsorb at liquid-gas or liquid-liquid interfaces

## Structure: Hydrophilic head + Hydrophobic tail
Energetically favourable to be at interface because of **amphiphilic** molecular structure. 

Surfactants adsorb at fluid interfaces:
![[b51d8690218bc86cdc5ab2545b4f60ed41f6db21d45182a31a10bdbbbe20581e.png]]
- **Surfactant oriented at interface**
	take a preferred orientation to ==minimise== unfavourable interactions between aqueous phase and hydrophobic tails
- **Random orientation in solution**


![[bebdc1b6c7d3134b40017ac2baf9e18626804ad54ee0c14fab42f6a6913819f9.png]]


## Head sizes and properties
-head group determines performance in applications
- **Nonionic** : stability in varying pH
- **Anionic** (-) : detergents, adsorption on polar surfaces 
- **Cationic** (+) : adsorption on surfaces

Most dirt and fibers surfaces carry a negative charge in water.
-> **Anionic surfactants are commonly used in detergents**

# Thermodynamics of interfaces
## Equilibrium partitioning of Surfactants
$\Gamma$ : surface concentration of surfactant $[moles/m^2]$
$c$ : surfactant concentration in bulk 
Gibbs adsorption equation: $$d\gamma = -\sum_i\Gamma_i d\mu_i$$relates change in surface tension to surface concentration and change in chemical potential of bulk surfactant solution. 

For one component: $$d\gamma=-\Gamma d\mu$$$$\mu=\mu_0+RT\ln a$$->  $$d\mu=RT\,d(\ln a)$$In dilute solution activity $a\approx c$ (concentration)
**Adsorption isotherm**: $$\Gamma=-\frac{1}{RT}\frac{d\gamma}{d\ln c}$$
From experimental data we can have plot $\gamma-\ln c$
![[bff30741d52ba837fd870da2c1263fbffff70275cf6473cf173e396a9db49b9a.png]]
After a certain point the slope decrease
-> **Critical Micellar concentration (CMC)**
--- Surfactants create ==**micelles**==  --- self-assembly

*\* Surfactant micelle solution -> viscosity change, etc.* 


## Decrease in surface tension
![[08d83f22461e47cb599468fa87cf6302e725ff74337ab17f872ddcb45ecafa59.png]]Surfactants decrease the surface tension: $$\gamma=\gamma_0-\Pi$$$\gamma$ : surface tension (function of surfactant concentration)
$\gamma_0$ : surface tension of fluid-fluid interface (no surfactants)
$\Pi$ : surface pressure of surfactant monolayer $[N/m]$


## Equation of state
---model to predict reduction of surface tension _vs_ surface concentration

- 2D ideal gas (point-like particles, no interactions) $$\Pi A=n^SRT\,\,\to\,\,\Pi=\Gamma RT$$
- finite-size particles (no interactions) $$\Pi(A-A_0)=n^SRT$$
- finite-size and van der Waals interactions $$(\Gamma-\frac{\alpha}{A^2})(A-A_0)=n^SRT$$
